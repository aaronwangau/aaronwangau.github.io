Gene Data Analysis Project

This is a self-initiated project aimed at building an automated pipeline for acquiring, processing, and refining large-scale healthcare datasets. The primary goal is to gain a deep understanding of the theoretical and technical foundations of clinical data collection, as well as the methodologies required for accurate data cleaning, integration, and transformation. By exploring real-world medical information workflows—including patient records, diagnostic measurements, and population-level health indicators—this project provides a practical pathway into the complexities of modern digital healthcare systems. Ultimately, it serves as a hands-on approach to understanding how data engineering, informatics, and clinical decision-support technologies can enhance nursing practice, improve care quality, and support evidence-based healthcare delivery.

Project Details

  • Data sources: ClinVar, ClinGen (notes), potential DisGeNET/OMIM expansion
  • Processing: VCF parsing, variant filtering, annotation
  • Outputs: Variant tables, plots, summary reports
